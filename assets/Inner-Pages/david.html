We All Mess Up:

Let’s be real—no developer gets it right on the first try. Or the second. Maybe the third. In this post, I’m breaking down five mistakes I made (some of them pretty bad 😅) as a full-stack developer, and how you can avoid them.


Steps:
  • Neglecting the Backend Early on, I focused way too much on front-end. My apps looked nice but crashed when they had to handle any logic or real data. Lesson? Backend is where the brains are—respect it.
  • Writing Monolithic Code I once wrote an entire app in a single 1000-line file. It worked—until it didn’t. Splitting logic into components, modules, and services made me a better developer overnight.
  • Not Using Git Properly Yes, I committed directly to main. Yes, I lost code. Yes, it hurt. Version control is your best friend. Branching and meaningful commit messages are a must.
  • Skipping Testing I used to say "it works on my machine!" Now I say, “does it pass the tests?” Unit and integration tests might feel like extra work, but they’ve saved me countless hours.
  • Not Asking for Help Sooner Stack Overflow, forums, Discord—use them. I used to struggle in silence. Now, I ask smarter questions and learn faster.tless hours.
Conclusion

Every mistake was a stepping stone. Hopefully, you’ll trip over fewer of them after reading this. If you’ve made your own mistakes, I’d love to hear them!
